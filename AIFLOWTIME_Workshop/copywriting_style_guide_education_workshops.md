# 1. PAS FRAMEWORK FOR COURSE SALES {#pas-framework}

## What is PAS?

**PAS = Problem → Agitate → Solution**

The PAS framework is one of the most reliable approaches to selling educational products. Unlike AIDA, which starts with grabbing attention, PAS focuses on identifying and addressing a specific problem. The problem itself becomes the hook that captures attention.

## The Three Steps Explained

### Step 1: PROBLEM
Identify a problem and present it to your reader. This is where you demonstrate empathy and show you understand their pain.

**Key Elements:**
- Be specific about the problem (not just "struggling with career" but "stuck in a role with no growth for 3 years")
- Use language your audience uses to describe their pain
- Make it relatable and immediate

**Course Sales Example:**
> "You've been in the same position for two years. Your colleagues have been promoted. You haven't. And every Monday morning, you feel that familiar knot in your stomach knowing another week of the same routine is starting."

### Step 2: AGITATE
Intensify the problem so it feels even worse than it seemed at first. This is the most critical step—pour salt on the wound (ethically) to heighten emotions.

**Key Elements:**
- Describe the ripple effects of the problem
- Paint a vivid picture of what happens if nothing changes
- Use emotional, sensory language
- Show the gap between where they are and where they want to be

**Course Sales Example:**
> "The longer you stay stuck, the more opportunities pass you by. That dream role? Someone else got it. The salary bump? It went to your colleague who took the certification you kept putting off. Meanwhile, your confidence erodes a little more each quarter review. You start questioning if you're even cut out for advancement."

### Step 3: SOLUTION
Present your course as the solution. Shift the mood to positive and show the transformation.

**Key Elements:**
- Introduce your course as the bridge to their desired outcome
- Focus on transformation, not just information
- Include specific, tangible results
- End with a clear call-to-action

**Course Sales Example:**
> "The Leadership Accelerator isn't just another course—it's the exact system that helped 340 professionals break through their career plateau in the last 18 months. In 8 weeks, you'll master the skills that get you noticed, build the confidence to speak up in rooms that matter, and create a promotion-ready portfolio that makes your next step inevitable. Enroll now and make this the quarter everything changes."

## PAS Formula Variations for Course Sales

### The "Question Loop" PAS (Copyhackers Style)
Start with a question that highlights the gap between desire and current pain:

> "What's the difference between professionals who get promoted every 18 months and those who stay stuck for years? It's not talent. It's not luck. It's having a proven system for demonstrating value—and that's exactly what you'll get in this program."

### The "Moment of Highest Tension" PAS
Describe a specific low point that your audience relates to:

> "It was 11 PM. Sarah was still at the office, finishing a presentation her manager had 'suggested' she redo. Again. She'd spent her weekend on it. Her dinner was cold on her desk. And she realized—this was her life now. Unless she did something different."

# 2. AIDA FRAMEWORK FOR COURSE SALES {#aida-framework}

## What is AIDA?

**AIDA = Attention → Interest → Desire → Action**

AIDA is one of the oldest and most well-known marketing frameworks, still highly relevant for course sales pages today.

## The Four Components Explained

### Step 1: ATTENTION
Grab the reader's attention with a headline or hook that addresses their pain points or interests.

**Key Elements:**
- Use curiosity, surprise, or direct problem statement
- Make it impossible to ignore
- Speak directly to your target audience

**Course Sales Headline Examples:**
- "Are you tired of watching others get promoted while you stay stuck?"
- "The real reason your career has plateaued (and how to fix it in 90 days)"
- "What if you could double your income without changing jobs?"

### Step 2: INTEREST
Build interest by explaining the benefits and results they can expect from your course.

**Key Elements:**
- Focus on outcomes, not just curriculum
- Use specific numbers and timeframes when possible
- Show you understand their specific challenges

**Course Sales Example:**
> "In this 6-week intensive, you'll learn the exact negotiation strategies that helped our students increase their salaries by an average of $23,000. Not theory—battle-tested scripts, email templates, and role-play exercises that work in real companies with real hiring managers."

### Step 3: DESIRE
Create desire by tapping into emotions and showing how your course solves their problems.

**Key Elements:**
- Paint the picture of life after the transformation
- Use testimonials and social proof
- Address objections before they arise
- Create FOMO (fear of missing out) ethically

**Course Sales Example:**
> "Imagine walking into your next performance review with confidence, knowing you have the skills and results to justify the promotion you've been working toward. Picture yourself leading projects that get noticed, speaking up in meetings with authority, and finally being seen as the leader you know you can be. That's what graduates of this program experience—and it's within your reach."

### Step 4: ACTION
Include a clear call-to-action that encourages enrollment.

**Key Elements:**
- Use action-oriented language
- Create urgency (without being manipulative)
- Remove friction from the decision
- Make the next step obvious

**Course Sales CTA Examples:**
- "Enroll now and start your transformation today"
- "Join 2,400+ professionals who've accelerated their careers"
- "Secure your spot—registration closes Friday at midnight"

## AIDA for Workshop Landing Pages

For workshops specifically, the AIDA structure often looks like:

1. **Attention:** Workshop title + compelling visual
2. **Interest:** What you'll learn (bullet points)
3. **Desire:** Who it's for + testimonials + what you'll walk away with
4. **Action:** Registration form with clear CTA button

# 3. BULLET POINT WRITING GUIDELINES {#bullet-points}

## The Golden Rule: Benefits First, Features Second

### Understanding the Difference

| **FEATURES** | **BENEFITS** |
|--------------|--------------|
| Describe what the product IS | Describe what the product DOES for the customer |
| Technical, specific | Solution-oriented, emotional |
| Logical appeal | Emotional + practical appeal |
| "8-week video course" | "Transform your skills in just 8 weeks without sacrificing your weekends" |
| "24/7 community access" | "Get unstuck fast with expert answers whenever you need them" |
| "Downloadable templates" | "Save hours of work with plug-and-play templates used by top performers" |

## The "So What?" Test

For every feature you write, ask "So what?" until you reach a benefit the customer actually cares about.

**Example Transformation:**
- Feature: "5 modules of video content"
- So what? "You'll learn comprehensive strategies"
- So what? "You'll have a complete roadmap to follow"
- **Final Benefit:** "Never wonder 'what should I do next?'—just follow the proven path laid out for you"

## Premium Brand Bullet Point Formulas

### Formula 1: The "You'll Get" Structure
> "You'll get [specific deliverable] so you can [specific benefit]"

**Example:**
> "You'll get the exact email scripts I use to land $10K+ clients, so you can start pitching with confidence even if you've never done it before"

### Formula 2: The "Even If" Structure (Objection Handler)
> "[Benefit]—even if [common objection]"

**Example:**
> "Master the fundamentals in just 30 minutes a day—even if you've never written a line of code before"

### Formula 3: The "Number + Outcome" Structure
> "[Number] [things] to [achieve outcome]"

**Example:**
> "7 battle-tested frameworks for handling any objection your prospects throw at you"

### Formula 4: The "Transformation" Structure
> "Go from [current state] to [desired state] with [specific method]"

**Example:**
> "Go from dreading presentations to commanding the room with our step-by-step confidence-building system"

## Bullet Point Do's and Don'ts

### DO:
- Lead with the benefit in bold or at the beginning
- Use specific numbers and timeframes
- Create curiosity (hint at value without giving everything away)
- Use power words: proven, tested, exclusive, step-by-step, exact
- Keep bullets scannable (2 lines max)
- Group related benefits together

### DON'T:
- Start with features without explaining the benefit
- Use generic claims without proof
- Make bullets so long they become paragraphs
- Use jargon your audience doesn't understand
- List more than 5-7 bullets in one section
- Write bullets that all sound the same

## Real Examples from Premium Brands

### MasterClass Style:
> "Learn at your own pace, with lifetime access to every lesson"
> "Download workbooks and resources to apply what you learn"
> "Watch on any device, anytime, anywhere"

### Ramit Sethi Style:
> "The exact word-for-word scripts to negotiate your salary (tested on thousands of students)"
> "The 'Hidden Job Market' strategy that 80% of people ignore"
> "How to answer 'What's your current salary?' without revealing your number"

### Copyhackers Style:
> "The 10x Sales Page Template that turns research into revenue"
> "Voice-of-Customer mining techniques that reveal exactly what your buyers want to hear"
> "The 'Slaughter the Sacred Cows' method for overcoming objections before they arise"

# 4. HEADLINE & SUBHEADLINE FORMULAS {#headlines}

## The 6-Figure Webinar Landing Page Formula

Based on extensive testing, this 3-section formula consistently hits 60-70% opt-in rates:

### Section 1: Hero Section (Above the Fold)

**Components:**
1. **Pre-headline:** Calls out your audience or frames the event (e.g., "FREE Workshop")
2. **Headline:** Leads with the benefit or transformation
3. **Whisper:** Parenthetical that handles objections or creates FOMO
4. **Sub-headline:** Explains exactly what they'll walk away with
5. **Social proof:** Date/time, duration, previous registrant count
6. **CTA button + countdown timer**

**Example:**
> **Pre-headline:** FREE Career Advancement Workshop
> **Headline:** Make This the Year You Finally Get Promoted
> **Whisper:** (Even if you've been passed over before)
> **Sub-headline:** Join 3,000+ professionals learning the 3-part system for standing out, getting noticed, and advancing your career—without working 60-hour weeks
> **Social proof:** Live Workshop | January 15th at 12 PM ET | 60 minutes
> **CTA:** [Save My Spot]

### Section 2: The "What You'll Get" Section

Header formula: "Here's what you're going to get out of this (free) [event type]..."

Bullet formula: Each item should have:
- A name/title
- A tangible outcome
- Bold phrases highlighting the most important parts

**Example:**
> **The Promotion Readiness Audit** — Discover exactly where you stand and what's holding you back from your next level
> **The Visibility Framework** — Learn how to get your work noticed by the people who matter (without being "that person" who constantly self-promotes)
> **The Career Conversation Script** — Get the exact words to use when asking for more responsibility, a raise, or a promotion

### Section 3: The "About Your Instructor" Section

Include:
- Quantifiable credentials in bold
- Relevant proof only (not life story)
- Professional photo
- Brief credibility statement

**Example:**
> "Over the past 8 years, I've helped **2,400+ professionals** advance their careers at companies like Google, Microsoft, and Deloitte. My work has been featured in Forbes, Harvard Business Review, and Fast Company. But more importantly, I've been exactly where you are—stuck, frustrated, and wondering if I'd ever break through."

## 15 High-Converting Headline Formulas

### 1. The How-To Formula
**Formula:** How to [Achieve Desired Outcome] in [Timeframe]

**Example:** "How to Negotiate a 20% Raise in Your Next Review"

### 2. The "Without" Formula (Objection Preempt)
**Formula:** How to [Achieve Outcome] Without [Pain Point]

**Example:** "How to Build Leadership Skills Without an MBA"

### 3. The Question Formula
**Formula:** [Question that taps into pain point or desire]?

**Example:** "Are You Making These 5 Career-Killing Mistakes?"

### 4. The Number Formula
**Formula:** [Number] [Proven/Secrets/Tactics] to [Desired Result]

**Example:** "7 Proven Strategies to Get Promoted Faster"

### 5. The Mistakes Formula
**Formula:** [Number] Mistakes [Audience] Make When [Task]

**Example:** "3 Mistakes High-Performers Make That Keep Them From Getting Promoted"

### 6. The Pattern Break Formula
**Formula:** Why [Counterintuitive Claim]

**Example:** "Why Working Harder Isn't Getting You Promoted (And What to Do Instead)"

### 7. The Case Study Formula
**Formula:** How [Person/Company] Got [Result] in [Timeframe]

**Example:** "How Sarah Went From Individual Contributor to Director in 18 Months"

### 8. The Value Proposition Formula
**Formula:** [Get/Achieve] [Primary Benefit] Without [Common Objection]

**Example:** "Get Executive Presence Without Changing Who You Are"

### 9. The Social Proof Formula
**Formula:** Join [Number]+ [Peers] Who [Outcome] with [Solution]

**Example:** "Join 5,000+ Professionals Who've Accelerated Their Careers With This System"

### 10. The Secrets Formula
**Formula:** The [Adjective] Secret to [Desired Outcome]

**Example:** "The Surprising Secret to Getting Noticed by Senior Leadership"

### 11. The Agitator Formula
**Formula:** Tired of [Pain Point]? There's a Better Way

**Example:** "Tired of Being Overlooked for Promotions? There's a Better Way"

### 12. The "Finally" Formula
**Formula:** Make [Timeframe] the Year You Finally [Desired Outcome]

**Example:** "Make 2025 the Year You Finally Get the Promotion You Deserve"

### 13. The Comparison Formula
**Formula:** [Current State] vs. [Desired State]: The [Solution] That Changes Everything

**Example:** "Invisible vs. In-Demand: The Career Strategy That Changes Everything"

### 14. The Urgency Formula
**Formula:** [Number] Days to [Outcome]: [What They'll Learn]

**Example:** "90 Days to Your Next Promotion: The Skills, Strategies, and Visibility You Need"

### 15. The Curiosity Gap Formula
**Formula:** What [Audience] Know About [Topic] That You Don't

**Example:** "What Promoted Professionals Know About Getting Noticed That You Don't"

## Subheadline Best Practices

### What Subheadlines Do:
- Explain the headline

- Create curiosity to keep reading

### Subheadline Formulas:

**1. The Expansion:**
> Headline: "Get Promoted Faster"

> Headline: "Master Public Speaking"

# 6. HYPE COPY VS. TRUST COPY {#hype-vs-trust}

## Understanding the Difference

| **HYPE COPY** | **TRUST COPY** |
|---------------|----------------|
| "Supercharge your success and skyrocket your results!" | "Stop spinning your wheels. Discover exactly where to focus your marketing to get more sales and less overwhelm." |
| "Unlock your potential and scale to 7 figures!" | "Pinpoint the exact bottlenecks slowing your growth and learn how to break through them." |
| "The ultimate blueprint to thrive in your industry!" | "Get the Focus Framework: the strategies behind global brand growth, made simple for small businesses." |
| "Revolutionary breakthrough that changes everything!" | "A proven system that helped 340 professionals advance their careers in the last 18 months." |
| "Make millions while you sleep!" | "Build a business that generates revenue even when you're not actively working." |

## Why Trust Copy Works Better for Professionals 25-45

### The Psychology:

1. **Skepticism is High**
- This demographic has seen countless "get rich quick" schemes
- They're tired of overpromising and underdelivering
- They have finely tuned "bullshit detectors"

2. **They Value Authenticity**
- They respond to honesty and transparency
- They want proof, not promises
- They research before buying

3. **They're Time-Poor**
- They don't have time to waste on things that don't work
- They want specific, actionable information
- They value efficiency over hype

4. **They're Results-Focused**
- They care about outcomes, not excitement
- They want to see evidence before committing
- They trust data over emotion

## The Six Pillars of Trust Copy

### 1. Solve a Real Problem
- Have a robust understanding of the business case behind your offer
- Do your research (then do more)
- Talk to real people in your target audience
- No amount of good marketing will solve a flawed business model

### 2. Use Concrete Language
- Say what you mean and mean what you say
- Avoid words like "elevate," "supercharge," "skyrocket"
- Specificity leads to authenticity
- Avoid exaggeration, half-truths, and hyperbole

### 3. Use Customer-Led Claims
- Don't use founder-led claims ("I work 4 hours a day and make 6 figures")
- Share your customers' actual results
- Example: "Our members have increased their revenue by 185%, profit by 206%, and grown their email list by 3%"
- This sets realistic expectations

### 4. Show, Don't Just Tell
- Use specific numbers and timeframes
- Include testimonials with real names and results
- Show screenshots, case studies, and proof
- Let customers speak for you

### 5. Handle Objections Honestly
- Acknowledge the real concerns your audience has
- Don't dismiss or minimize their fears
- Provide genuine solutions to their objections
- Be transparent about limitations

### 6. Make Promises You Can Keep
- Don't promise results you can't guarantee
- Be clear about what the course requires from them
- Set realistic expectations
- Under-promise and over-deliver

## Hype Copy Red Flags (NEVER Use)

### Words to Avoid:
- Supercharge
- Skyrocket
- Explode (your growth/results)
- Revolutionary
- Breakthrough
- Game-changing
- Instantly
- Effortlessly
- Guaranteed (unless you can actually guarantee it)
- Secret
- Trick
- Hack
- Magic

### Phrases to Avoid:
- "Make money while you sleep"
- "Get rich quick"
- "No experience necessary"
- "Anyone can do this"
- "Passive income on autopilot"
- "The only system you'll ever need"
- "Never work again"
- "Financial freedom in 30 days"

## Trust Copy Green Lights (ALWAYS Use)

### Words to Use:
- Proven
- Tested
- Specific
- Exact
- Step-by-step
- System
- Framework
- Method
- Strategy
- Research-backed
- Data-driven

### Phrases to Use:
- "Here's exactly what you'll get..."
- "This is what worked for [specific number] of our students..."
- "In [timeframe], you'll learn..."
- "The same system that helped [person] achieve [result]..."
- "Based on [number] years of research/experience..."
- "Here's what that looks like in practice..."

## The "Help, Don't Hype" Test

Before publishing any copy, ask:

1. **Am I helping or hyping?**
   - Is this focused on solving their problem or making my product sound amazing?

2. **Can I prove this claim?**
   - Do I have evidence, data, or testimonials to back this up?

3. **Would I say this to a friend?**
   - If I were giving advice to someone I care about, would I use these words?

4. **Is this specific or vague?**
   - Can I replace vague claims with specific numbers, timeframes, or examples?

5. **Am I creating FOMO ethically?**
   - Is the urgency real (limited seats, closing date) or manufactured?

# 7. COPYWRITER'S CHECKLIST {#checklist}

## Pre-Writing Checklist

### Audience Understanding
- [ ] I've identified the specific audience segment
- [ ] I know their #1 pain point
- [ ] I understand their desired outcome
- [ ] I know the objections they have
- [ ] I understand the language they use to describe their problems

### Research
- [ ] I've reviewed customer testimonials and feedback
- [ ] I've analyzed competitor sales pages
- [ ] I've identified the unique selling proposition
- [ ] I've gathered specific numbers and results
- [ ] I have proof points (testimonials, case studies, data)

## Writing Checklist

### Headlines
- [ ] The headline passes the "3-second test" (reader knows what it's about immediately)
- [ ] The headline includes a benefit or addresses a pain point
- [ ] The subheadline explains or expands on the headline
- [ ] Both are specific, not vague
- [ ] They create curiosity to keep reading

### PAS Framework (if using)
- [ ] The problem is specific and relatable
- [ ] The agitation intensifies the problem (without being manipulative)
- [ ] The solution is presented as the bridge to transformation
- [ ] The transition between sections is smooth

### AIDA Framework (if using)
- [ ] Attention is grabbed in the first 3 seconds
- [ ] Interest is built through benefits and outcomes
- [ ] Desire is created through emotion and proof
- [ ] Action is clear and frictionless

### Bullet Points
- [ ] Every bullet leads with a benefit
- [ ] Features are only included with benefit explanation
- [ ] Bullets pass the "So what?" test
- [ ] They're scannable (2 lines max each)
- [ ] Power words are used appropriately

### Body Copy
- [ ] Every section earns its place
- [ ] Each paragraph moves the reader closer to action
- [ ] Objections are handled before they arise
- [ ] Social proof is woven throughout
- [ ] The tone matches the audience

### CTA (Call-to-Action)
- [ ] The CTA uses action-oriented language
- [ ] It's clear what happens when they click
- [ ] Urgency is created ethically (if applicable)
- [ ] The button copy is benefit-focused
- [ ] There are multiple CTAs throughout the page

## Post-Writing Checklist

### The "So What? Prove It" Test
- [ ] Every claim answers "So what?"
- [ ] Every claim is backed by proof
- [ ] No unsupported superlatives (best, greatest, amazing)

### The 4 Cs Test
- [ ] Is it **Clear**? (No jargon, easy to understand)
- [ ] Is it **Concise**? (No unnecessary words)
- [ ] Is it **Compelling**? (Creates interest and desire)
- [ ] Is it **Credible**? (Backed by proof)

### The 4 Us Test
- [ ] Is it **Useful**? (Solves a real problem)
- [ ] Is there **Urgency**? (Reason to act now)
- [ ] Is it **Unique**? (Different from competitors)
- [ ] Is it **Ultra-specific**? (Numbers, examples, details)

### The "Hype Check"
- [ ] No hype words (supercharge, skyrocket, etc.)
- [ ] No unrealistic promises
- [ ] No manufactured urgency
- [ ] Concrete language throughout
- [ ] Customer-led claims, not founder-led

### The "Trust Check"
- [ ] Specific numbers and timeframes used
- [ ] Testimonials with real names and results
- [ ] Objections handled honestly
- [ ] Promises are realistic
- [ ] Transparency about what the course requires

### The "Read Aloud" Test
- [ ] Copy has been read out loud
- [ ] It sounds natural and conversational
- [ ] No awkward phrases or tongue-twisters
- [ ] The rhythm flows well

### The "Friend" Test
- [ ] Would I say this to a friend?
- [ ] Does it sound like advice or a sales pitch?
- [ ] Is the tone appropriate?

## Final Review Checklist

### Technical
- [ ] No typos or grammatical errors
- [ ] Consistent formatting
- [ ] Proper use of bold, italics, and capitalization
- [ ] All links work

## FINAL NOTES FOR COPYWRITERS

### Remember:

1. **Your job is to help, not hype.** Every word should serve the reader.

2. **Specificity beats superlatives.** "Increased revenue by 47%" beats "amazing results."

3. **Trust is earned, not claimed.** Use proof, not promises.

4. **The best copy sounds like a conversation.** Read it aloud. If it sounds weird, rewrite it.

5. **Test everything.** What works for one audience may not work for another.

6. **When in doubt, cut it out.** Shorter is usually better.

7. **Your reader is the hero.** The course is the tool. You're the guide.

8. **Emotion drives decisions.** Logic justifies them. Use both.

9. **Objections are opportunities.** Address them head-on.

10. **The goal is one reader, one action.** Write to one person, not a crowd.
